Main Administrative Buildings in the City

Description and links of main administrative buildings in the city

  • New York Police Department (NYPD)

    The NYPD Headquarters, known as One Police Plaza, is near City Hall and represents the history and evolution of law enforcement in NYC.
  • Fire Department of New York (FDNY)

    Visit the FDNY Fire Zone in Rockefeller Center, offering interactive exhibits and insights into NYC's fire safety legacy.
  • New York City Council

    Located at City Hall, this historic site is a must-see for its architecture and role in NYC governance since the 1800s.
  • Office of the Mayor

    Situated in City Hall, a National Historic Landmark and a key stop on Civic Center walking tours.
  • NYC Health + Hospitals (HHC)

    Bellevue Hospital, part of NYC Health + Hospitals, is the oldest public hospital in the United States, with historic significance.
  • Department of Sanitation (DSNY)

    The NYC Sanitation Museum offers a quirky yet informative look into how NYC has managed waste over the centuries.
  • Department of Cultural Affairs (DCLA)

    Oversees cultural institutions like The Met, MoMA, and more—perfect for exploring NYC’s rich artistic heritage.
  • Mayor's Action Center

    An administrative hub located near City Hall, often included in downtown NYC tours.
  • Mayor's Public Engagement Unit (PEU)

    Works closely with public events; some are hosted in landmark locations around NYC.
  • Mayor's Office of Immigrant Affairs (MOIA)

    Significant for its historical role in NYC as a melting pot of cultures; the office is often linked to Ellis Island tours.
  • Office to End Domestic and Gender-Based Violence

    Works with organizations that may feature exhibitions or events in landmark locations promoting awareness.
  • Mayor's Office of Citywide Event Coordination and Management (CECM)

    Plays a key role in organizing major NYC events like the Thanksgiving Parade, adding to the city’s vibrant culture.
  • NYC & Company

    NYC’s official tourism organization, offering visitor information and guides from its central Manhattan office.
  • Mayor's Office of Ethnic and Community Media

    Promotes multicultural events and celebrations across the city, many held at iconic NYC landmarks.
  • Office of Payroll Administration (OPA)

    Located in a historic building, this office is sometimes included in architectural tours of lower Manhattan.
