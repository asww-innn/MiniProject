Public Transport in New York City

Transportation system of the city and links to maps

Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA)

The MTA is New York City’s comprehensive public transportation system, including subways, buses, and ferries, with most services operating 24/7.

Subways

The subway system features 24 lines, marked by letters or numbers, connecting Manhattan, Bronx, Brooklyn, and Queens. Stops cover most areas you’ll need to reach.

Buses

NYC has 253 local and 71 express routes. Stops are marked with signs displaying the route and schedule, making navigation straightforward.

Regional Trains

For travel beyond the boroughs, MTA trains connect NYC to Long Island, Westchester, Hudson Valley, and Connecticut. Details are available on the MTA website.

Tickets

Purchase MTA cards at any station. Options include single rides, unlimited passes, or pay-per-use cards. Retain your card to reload if necessary.

Hop-On Hop-Off Tours

Big Bus tours combine sightseeing and transit, letting you explore the city’s landmarks while planning your itinerary. Admission is often included in city passes.

Walking and Biking

New York’s pedestrian-friendly layout makes walking or biking ideal for exploring attractions like Central Park and the waterfront. Rentals and tours are widely available.

Taxis and Ridesharing

Yellow taxis and ridesharing services are convenient. Keep in mind that some taxis accept only cash and toll fares may apply.

Getting to New York City

By Plane

  • JFK Airport: Located 15 miles from Manhattan, accessible via AirTrain and public transport.
  • Newark Airport: 16 miles away, often faster than JFK with similar taxi fares.
  • LaGuardia Airport: Only 8 miles from Manhattan, offering shorter taxi rides.

By Train

Amtrak serves NYC’s Penn Station, providing connections to major cities along the Northeast Corridor.

By Bus

Affordable options like Megabus, FlixBusUSA, and Greyhound drop passengers at key landmarks across Manhattan.
